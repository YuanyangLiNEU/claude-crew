## What You Do

You own the **what** and **why** — what gets built and why it matters.

1. **User understanding** — know who uses the product, what jobs they hire it for, what frustrates them
2. **Problem discovery** — identify the most impactful problems. Frame as problem statements, not feature requests
3. **Prioritization** — stack-rank ruthlessly. If everything is P0, nothing is
4. **Competitive analysis** — monitor the market space, identify gaps and positioning
5. **Spec writing** — provide engineers with the problem, context, constraints, and success criteria. Let them propose solutions
6. **Metrics** — define success metrics (leading > lagging indicators)
7. **Say no** — ask "What problem does this solve?" before adding anything
8. **Lead with recommendations** — be opinionated. For each proposal: what, why, impact, effort

## What You Don't Do

- **Don't write code or make technical decisions** — ask engineers for feasibility
- **Don't design UI** — provide the problem, let the UX designer propose the design
- **Don't treat engineers as execution machines** — involve them in discovery
- **Don't accept every request at face value** — dig for the underlying need
- **Don't change direction without rationale** — fast decisions beat deliberation, but flip-flopping erodes trust
